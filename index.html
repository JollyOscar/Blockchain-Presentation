Real‑World Adoption

Blockchain shows up most where shared truth reduces delays, paperwork, and the need for middlemen.

Banking & Financial Services

  • International transfers can drop from days to minutes.
  • Reduces reliance on middlemen banks for transaction verification.
  • Often available 24/7 instead of business-hours-only systems.

Government / Public Sector

  • Sweden uses blockchain for land registry, replacing a slow, paper-heavy process.
  • Helps reduce manual verification and paperwork.
  • Estimated savings: €100M (~$162M CAD) annually.
Why this adoption matters

When multiple organizations must agree on the same record, blockchain can reduce delays by providing a shared, auditable ledger. That’s why it shows up in payments and public records.

Pros & Cons Today

Pros (What it solves well)

  • Instant settlement and 24/7 markets for digital assets.
  • Eliminating middlemen: shared ledgers reduce costly reconciliation & admin overhead.
  • Data integrity: timestamps help prove original source in an era of deepfakes.
  • Trust in AI: immutable audit trails for training data and model provenance.

Cons (Challenges & criticisms)

  • Scalability: Layer‑2 helps (Polygon/Starknet), but base layers can still bottleneck.
  • Regulation: 2025/2026 rules (e.g., EU MiCA) pushed out “wild west” actors.
  • UX complexity: wallets, keys, and onboarding remain barriers for many users.
  • Energy: some networks are energy-heavy (depends on consensus design).
Gaming note: the focus has shifted toward portable ownership (items/skins across ecosystems) instead of pure “Play‑to‑Earn”.
Slide 4

Our Verdict: YES

Why it still matters

  • Blockchain has graduated from “emerging” to foundational.
  • In 2026, it’s part of the internet stack for value + identity.
  • It helps close the trust gap created by AI and deepfakes.

One-line summary

“Blockchain is the new plumbing: mostly invisible, but essential for systems that need shared truth.”

(Relevant in IT/networking much like cloud computing is today.)

The “Surprising” Insight

2026

AI Wallets

AI “agents” can have their own blockchain wallets to pay for services (server costs, API calls, micro‑payments) without a human approving every transaction.

Why it matters
Enables autonomous, auditable machine-to-machine commerce.
What it needs
Strong security, policy controls, and clear regulation.
The big idea
Identity + payments become programmable “building blocks”.
